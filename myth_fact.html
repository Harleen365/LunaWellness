Fact:
Periods are a biological process that begins well before sexual maturity and are completely independent of virginity.

Myth:
Periods sync up with those around you if you spend a lot of time together.

Fact:
The cervix, the opening to the uterus, is very small. A tampon cannot get lost inside you; it simply sits in the lower vagina.

Myth:
Periods Signal the Loss of Virginity:
In some societies, menstruation is mistakenly linked to sexual activity.

Fact:
Spicy food might irritate your digestive system and cause cramps, but it doesn't directly affect your period flow.

Myth:
Tampons Can Get Lost Inside You:
This fear can be a deterrent for some.

Fact:
While the terms "menstruation" and "lunar" share a root word, the human menstrual cycle (21-35 days) is regulated by hormones, not lunar phases.

Myth:
Spicy Food Makes Your Period Heavier or More Painful:
This is a common concern, especially in cultures with spicy cuisine.

Fact:

While some animals might be drawn to blood scent in general, there's no evidence they specifically target menstruating women. Periods are a natural process, not a beacon for mythical creatures!

Myth:
The Moon Cycle Dictates Your Period:
This belief ties menstruation to the lunar cycle (around 28 days) and suggests periods should perfectly sync with the full moon.
