Effects of PCOD/PCOS

So Here are some effects of PCOD/PCOS

PCOD (Polycystic Ovarian Disease) or PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) is a hormonal disorder common among women of reproductive age. It can cause irregular menstrual periods, excessive hair growth, acne, and obesity.

Let's Study these Effects of PCOD/PCOS in detail

  • Irregular periods:Irregular periods are one of the hallmark symptoms of PCOD/PCOS. Women with PCOD/PCOS often experience menstrual cycles that are longer than 35 days, fewer than eight menstrual cycles a year, or periods that are very heavy when they do occur. This irregularity is caused by hormonal imbalances that affect the ovaries' ability to release eggs properly.

  • Heavy bleeding: PCOS/PCOD can lead to heavy menstrual bleeding because hormonal imbalances and irregular ovulation cause the uterine lining to build up excessively. When menstruation finally occurs, the shedding of this thickened lining results in heavier than normal bleeding.

  • Hair growth:PCOS/PCOD can cause increased hair growth (hirsutism) in areas such as the face, chest, and back due to elevated androgen levels. Conversely, it can also lead to hair thinning or loss on the scalp (androgenic alopecia) due to the same hormonal imbalances.

  • Acne:In PCOS/PCOD, elevated androgen levels can contribute to the development of acne by increasing sebum production and influencing follicular keratinization, leading to clogged pores and inflammation, particularly around the face, chest, and back.

  • Weight gain:Weight gain in PCOS/PCOD is often influenced by insulin resistance, hormonal imbalances (such as elevated androgens), and genetic factors, leading to difficulties in regulating weight and increased accumulation of abdominal fat.

  • Darkening of the skin: In PCOS/PCOD, darkening of the skin (acanthosis nigricans) can occur due to insulin resistance, leading to hyperinsulinemia. This condition typically manifests as velvety, thickened, and darkened skin patches, often seen in body folds like the neck, armpits, and groin.

Management of PCOD/PCOS

While there is no cure for PCOD/PCOS, several strategies can help manage the symptoms:

  • Healthy Diet:Focus on a balanced diet with low glycemic index foods to help manage insulin resistance. This includes whole grains, lean proteins, fruits, vegetables, and healthy fats.

  • Regular exercise:Physical activity helps improve insulin sensitivity, manage weight, and reduce symptoms.

  • Weight Management:Achieving and maintaining a healthy weight can improve hormone balance and reduce symptoms.

  • Birth Contro Pills: Oral contraceptives can regulate menstrual cycles and reduce androgen levels.

  • Regular Monitoring and Support:Regular check-ups with healthcare providers are important to monitor symptoms, hormone levels, and overall health.

  • Surgical Interventions: In cases where other treatments have not been effective, ovarian drilling (laparoscopic ovarian diathermy) may be recommended to induce ovulation in women with PCOS.
